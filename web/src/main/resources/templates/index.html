Letter from the Secretaries-General

Dear Esteemed Delegates and Advisors,

On behalf of the Brown Model United Nations program, we are honored to invite you to the Twenty-Second Annual Session of the Brown University Simulation of the United Nations (BUSUN), which will be held on the Brown University campus from November 9-11, 2018. We are proud to say that BUSUN will welcome over 900 students and 100 advisors to our campus in November. While BUSUN continues to grow and evolve each year, we are excited to continue some of the traditions that we believe make our conference special. Delegations return to year after year because of our small committee sizes, informed and passionate chairs, and welcoming atmosphere. As a Secretariat and staff, we value diplomacy and do our best to create a productive and positive experience for all delegates and advisors.

We are thrilled to announce that the conference theme for BUSUN XXII will be global health. Delegates will explore not only more traditional aspects of global health — including accessibility, equity, and affordability of care — but also the interconnectivity of human health and wellness with other topics including bioterrorism, food security, and the rights of women. We are confident that delegates at BUSUN this year, like those who have attended BUSUN in the past, will participate in engaging debate that challenges their understanding of current events and historical topics.

On behalf of the BUSUN Secretariat and our entire staff, we hope that you will be able to join us in November. Please contact our Director of External Affairs, Elinor Martinez, at info@busun.org with any questions or concerns. We look forward to welcoming you to BUSUN 2018!

Best,
Colette Bare and Jourdan Smithwick
Secretaries-General, BUSUN XXII
sg@busun.org
